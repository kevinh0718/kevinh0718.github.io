Deep learning-based aberration compensation

Addressing skull property uncertainties in transcranial PACT

Skull properties are practically unavailalbe, but property value uncertainties can result in severe imaging artifacts which limits the application of transcranial PACT human imaging. Combining attention Unet and realistically synthesized dataset, the learning-based method successfully mitigate the artifacts in images of experimental data that involved and adult human skull. The fact that the method was solely trained using synthetic data but succeeded on experimental data demonstrating the applicability of deep learning in advanced image reconstruction methods [1][6][8].

Deep learning-based deep tissue signal enhancement

Segmentaion for target-specific signal enhancement

Optical attenuation leads to low SNR in deep tissue PA imaging. With the use of multiple conditional generative adversarial networks, the tissue boundary was segmented from adjunct ultrasound images and predict the optical fluence. Then, the signal of learning-based segmented blood vessel was enhanced, leading to deep tissue-specific SNR enhancement. This project demonstrates the use of deep learning methods in computer vision tasks.

Gold nano particles for contrast enhanced imaging

Biomimetic membrane enabled cell-specific contrast enhancement in both SERS and PACT

By leveraging gold nanostars with biomimetic-membrane as dual-modality contrast agents, deeper tissue penetration was enabled for PAI, providing precise tumor localization beneath layers of skin and fat. The integration of PAI with surface-enhanced Raman scattering (SERS) further enhances tumor identification and differentiation, facilitating image-guided tumor resections. These findings highlight the potential of biomimetic nanoparticles for improving intraoperative cancer detection and surgical precision.

Deep Learning-guided imaging scanning system

Segmentation to determine object surface for guiding the scanning path.

With use of conditional GAN for tissue segmentaion based on ultrasound imaging, the photoacoustic imaging scanning path, controled by robotic arm, was optimized so that the maximum optical energy focusing and the acoustic focusing could apply to the targeted imaging depth for arbitrary object shape. This leads to improved optical energy delivery and also trustworthy depth encoding when analizing the morphology of specimen.

Joint reconstruction of skull wave speed and p0

Model-based method for addressing skull property uncertainties in transcranial PACT

In addition to the learning-based method for compesnating skull property uncertainties-induced imaging artifacts, a physics-informed model-based approach was also developed and evaluated. This method leverages gradient-free optimization of skull wave speeds to significantly reduce computational burden, making previously infeasible large-scale computations practical [7].

FEM and FDTD modeling of human skull

Multiple numerical scheme for efficient skull modelling

Image reconstruction in transcranial PACT is challenging and often relies on a skull model to characterize skull properties. Both the finite element method (FEM) and the finite-difference time-domain (FDTD) method were evaluated, each with distinct advantages and limitations. FEM allowed for efficient multi-domain modeling, reducing the required degrees of freedom, while FDTD benefited from straightforward implementation of parallel computing techniques for acceleration [2].
